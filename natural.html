How to Select the Right Drying Oil For Your Painting

Refined vs. Cold Pressed vs. Stand Oils

Dear {{ first_name|default:'fellow artist' }},

In oil painting, every brush stroke and color choice carries the weight of your vision. But have you considered the foundational role that drying oils play in bringing your artwork to life? The right drying oil can not only enhance the quality of your painting but also ensure its longevity and brilliance.

We at Natural Pigments understand the pivotal role materials play in the art-making process. This is why we're excited to share with you an invaluable resource that sheds light on the nuanced world of drying oils. Our latest article, "Choosing the Right Drying Oil for Your Art," delves into the science and artistry behind selecting the perfect oil to complement your painting practices.

Drying oils are a binder in oil paints, affecting drying times, consistency, and the final sheen of your work. With a myriad of options available, from linseed to walnut and beyond, each oil brings its unique characteristics to the canvas. Our guide aims to empower you with the knowledge to make informed choices about which oil best suits your artistic needs and preferences.

Why is this important? The right drying oil can dramatically influence the texture, drying time, and overall appearance of your painting. Whether you're a seasoned professional or a budding artist, understanding these subtleties can help to ensure that every piece you create is a true reflection of your intended vision.

We invite you to explore this guide and discover:

  • The properties and benefits of various drying oils

  • How to match the right oil with your painting style and technique

  • Tips for experimenting with different oils to achieve your desired effect

  • Let this article be your compass in the vast sea of artistic materials, guiding you to make choices that enhance the integrity and beauty of your work.

Read the full article here: How To Choose The Right Drying Oil For Your Art

Your art deserves the best. Equip yourself with the knowledge to choose wisely, and watch as your paintings transform before your eyes.

Warm regards,

George O'Hanlon and Tatiana Zaytseva
Cofounders
